## FAQ Prep (for comments)

**Q: How is this different from MFP?**
A: Three key differences:

1. **Speed** - MFP is bloated with 47 features (social, recipes, challenges). FitTrack has 3 features (log, track, progress). Logging a meal takes 30 seconds, not 2+ minutes.

2. **Pricing honesty** - MFP removed the barcode scanner from free and now charges $80/year for features that used to be included. FitTrack is $10/month, all features included, no surprises.

3. **Target user** - MFP tries to serve everyone (beginners, casual dieters, athletes). FitTrack is built specifically for experienced lifters who know their macros and just want fast tracking.

**Comparison:**
- **MyFitnessPal:** 47 features, you use 3
- **FitTrack:** 3 features, you'll use 3

If you're past the beginner phase and tracking feels like homework, FitTrack is for you.

**Q: Why $10/month?**
A: That's how I keep it ad-free and privacy-focused. MFP is "free" because they monetize your data. FitTrack doesn't.

**Q: Mobile app?**
A: Not yet - web version is mobile-responsive. Native apps coming if there's demand.

**Q: What's on the roadmap?**
A: Building based on user feedback. Right now it's intentionally minimal. If people want specific features, I'll prioritize them.

## Tips for Product Hunt Success

1. **Launch on Tuesday-Thursday** (most traffic)
2. **Reply fast** (first 6 hours are critical)
3. **Be genuine** (PH community hates fake/salesy)
4. **Ask for feedback** (not just votes)
5. **Engage with other launches** (support others, they'll support you)
6. **Don't vote-brigade** (PH will penalize you)
7. **Have a backup plan** (if you don't hit #1, you can re-launch in 6 months with improvements)

**Success metrics:**
- Top 10 of the day = great
- Top 5 = excellent
- #1 = unicorn (don't expect it first try)

Even if you don't rank high, PH brings quality traffic and feedback. Win either way.
