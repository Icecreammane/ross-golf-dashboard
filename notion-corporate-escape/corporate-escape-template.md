### Risk Assessment

#### What Could Go Wrong?

**Risk 1: Projects don't generate revenue fast enough**

- *Mitigation:* Build valuable skills (AI, automation, product), maintain network, keep LinkedIn active.

- *Mitigation:* Emergency fund separate from move fund, health insurance plan, stay fit.

### Recovery Activities (The "Refuel" List)

When energy drops below 5/10, pick one:

- [ ] **Full rest day** (no side project work)
- [ ] **Nature walk** (30+ min, no phone)
- [ ] **Social time** (friends who energize you)
- [ ] **Creative play** (hobby with zero pressure)
- [ ] **Extra sleep** (9+ hours, guilt-free)

**Rule:** One recovery activity per week minimum. Two if grinding hard.

### Work-Life Balance During Transition

**The "Hell Yes or No" Filter:**
- Corporate job? Yes (for now, it's funding freedom)
- Side projects? Hell yes (building the future)
- Social obligation that drains you? **NO.**
- Relationship that matters? Hell yes
- Mindless scrolling? **NO.**
- Fitness? Hell yes (non-negotiable)

**Protect your energy like your life depends on it. Because your escape does.**

---

### Dopamine Defense Tactics

**The Problem:** Corporate job = boring. Brain craves dopamine hits (social media, junk food, Netflix binges). These kill progress.

**The Solution: Intentional Dopamine**

**High-Value Dopamine (Do More):**
- ‚úÖ Shipping a feature
- ‚úÖ First customer
- ‚úÖ Workout PR
- ‚úÖ Savings milestone hit
- ‚úÖ Skill breakthrough

**Low-Value Dopamine (Do Less):**
- ‚ùå Infinite scroll (Twitter, Instagram, TikTok)
- ‚ùå Junk food binges
- ‚ùå Binge-watching TV
- ‚ùå Online shopping
- ‚ùå Arguing on the internet

**Tactics:**
1. **Delete social apps** from phone (browser only, intentional use)
2. **Block distracting sites** during work hours (Freedom, Cold Turkey)
3. **Reward system:** Hit goal ‚Üí guilt-free fun
4. **Replace bad habits:** Craving scroll? Do 10 pushups instead

## üß† MENTAL GAME

### Mindset Shifts Needed

**From ‚Üí To:**
- Corporate job = identity ‚Üí Corporate job = **temporary funding source**
- Security = steady paycheck ‚Üí Security = **skills + savings + options**
- Success = promotion ‚Üí Success = **freedom + impact + fulfillment**
- Risk = scary ‚Üí Risk = **calculated, managed, necessary**
- Failure = permanent ‚Üí Failure = **data, lesson, redirect**

---

### Dealing with Imposter Syndrome

**The Voice:** "Who are you to build products? You're just a [job title]. You're not a real entrepreneur."

**The Truth:**
- ‚úÖ Every entrepreneur started as "not a real entrepreneur"
- ‚úÖ You're learning by doing (the only way that works)
- ‚úÖ Your background is an advantage (you understand corporate customers)
- ‚úÖ Done is better than perfect
- ‚úÖ You don't need permission to build

**When It Hits:**
1. **List your wins** (no matter how small)
2. **Remember why** (read your motivation section)
3. **Ship anyway** (action kills doubt)

---

### Handling Uncertainty

**What You Can't Control:**
- Market response to your products
- Economic conditions
- Other people's opinions
- Timing of success

**What You CAN Control:**
- **Showing up daily** (consistency > intensity)
- **Shipping projects** (velocity > perfection)
- **Learning from failures** (pivot fast)
- **Managing energy** (fitness, rest, focus)
- **Building skills** (invest in yourself)
- **Saving money** (every dollar = freedom closer)

**Focus on the controllables. Let the rest go.**

---

### Staying Motivated When Progress Is Slow

**The Reality:** Most overnight successes take years. Progress is not linear.

**Motivation Strategies:**

1. **Track micro-wins:** 
   - Coded today? Win.
   - Shipped a feature? Win.
   - Saved $100? Win.
   - Worked out? Win.

2. **Visualize the future:**
   - What does your first day in Florida feel like?
   - What time do you wake up when you control your schedule?
   - Who are you when you're free?

3. **Compare to past-you, not others:**
   - Where were you 3 months ago?
   - What have you built since then?
   - How much closer are you to $500 MRR?

4. **Join the struggle:**
   - Find others escaping corporate
   - Share the journey
   - Accountability > motivation

5. **Remember the alternative:**
   - 5 more years of the same corporate grind
   - Never taking the shot
   - Regret > failure

**You're not behind. You're in the arena. Keep going.**
